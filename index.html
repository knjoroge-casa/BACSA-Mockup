Connecting Capability with National Priorities

South Africa faces sophisticated organised crime networks that exploit systemic weaknesses. BACSA connects private sector capability with government priorities—providing coordination, technology, and expertise where they're needed most.

Three Strategic Pillars

Rule of Law

Supporting judicial and law enforcement institutions to function effectively and independently.

Illicit Economy and Organised Crime

Targeting the systems and networks that enable corruption, extortion, and criminal enterprise.

Innovation and Incubation

Deploying technology and new approaches where traditional methods fall short.

Six Core Functions

1

Strategic Platform

A stable, professional channel for private sector expertise to support rule-of-law strengthening.

2

Intelligence & Insight

Translating complex institutional developments into clear guidance for business leaders and investors.

3

Technology Coordination

Deploying AI, data analytics, and digital forensics capabilities to support law enforcement.

4

Alignment & Integration

Directing resources and expertise to identified systemic weaknesses and national priorities.

5

Clear Boundaries

Helping institutions operate more effectively without crossing into prosecution or enforcement decision-making.

6

Delivery Discipline

Measurable outcomes, defined timelines, and Board-level oversight on every initiative.
